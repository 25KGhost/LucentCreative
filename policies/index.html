Effective Date:Immediately upon engagement
Governing Scope:All services, deliverables, communications, agreements, and interactions with Lucient Creative (“the Agency”).

Article 1 — Purpose

These Terms exist to define how we work, what clients can expect, and the standards we uphold.

  • They protect the client, the Agency, and the integrity of the project.
  • Once a client initiates a project, these Terms become binding.

Article 2 — Engagement & Scope of Work

2.1 Project Initiation

A project begins when:

  • The client accepts the proposal or package.
  • The client pays the initial 50% deposit.
  • The Agency confirms the start.
2.2 Scope Definition

The scope includes only what is listed in the proposal or package. Anything beyond that is considered out-of-scope and billed separately.

Article 3 — Pricing

Our pricing is not a fixed one-size-fits-all formula — it’s a reflection of the exact work your brand needs to move with clarity and confidence.

3.1 Fixed Project Fee

Once a project begins, the agreed price is **locked**.

3.2 Scope Changes

The fee will not change unless the client expands the scope or requests new work beyond what was originally planned. Any change requiring additional work will be quoted and approved separately.

3.3 Post-Delivery Costs

After the project is delivered, additional costs only apply if the client requests ongoing maintenance, revisions, or new deliverables.

Article 4 — Payments & Fees

4.1 Payment Structure
  • 50% upfront before any work begins.
  • 50% on delivery of the final project.
4.2 Refunds

Once work has started, no refunds are offered under any circumstance. The deposit compensates for reserved time, planning, design, and execution.

4.3 Late Payments

There are no late fees, but work halts until payment is received. Ownership is not transferred until full payment is complete.

Article 5 — Revisions

5.1 Included Revisions

Each project includes three (3) revision rounds. A revision is defined as a set of changes submitted at once.

5.2 Additional Revisions

Any revisions beyond the included three are billed at the Agency’s standard rate. The Agency will inform the client before any additional cost is applied.

Article 6 — Timelines & Delivery

6.1 Standard Timelines

The Agency provides estimated delivery dates based on the approved scope.

6.2 Client Delays

If the client fails to provide content, materials, or feedback, timelines extend automatically.

6.3 Agency Delays

In rare cases of critical circumstances or emergencies affecting the Agency or team members, delivery dates may be extended. The Agency will notify the client as early as possible.

6.4 Fast-Track Requests

Rush or priority delivery may incur additional fees.

Article 7 — Confidentiality & Data Protection

7.1 Built-In NDA

By working with the Agency, both parties agree to a mutual non-disclosure commitment. All confidential information, internal business details, credentials, and data shared remain private.

7.2 Sensitive Data Handling

The Agency:

  • Stores client credentials securely.
  • Shares data only with essential team members.
  • Uses client information solely for the project.
  • Never sells, transfers, or exposes client data.
7.3 Data Deletion
  • If the client requests ongoing maintenance or revisions: data is deleted 60 days after the final update or maintenance window.
  • If not: data is deleted immediately upon project delivery.

This applies to logins, internal files, drafts, credentials, and sensitive documents.

Article 8 — Portfolio Usage & Displays

8.1 Public Work

The Agency can publicly show: Screenshots of the final website, non-sensitive visuals, design elements, and general layout.

8.2 Private Showcasing

The Agency may privately show real client work to other potential clients as reference.

8.3 Protection of Client Identity

Unless given explicit permission:

  • The Agency will not use the client’s real text, brand name, logo, or domain publicly.
  • Public showcases use demo text, placeholder content, or safe variants.

This protects the client’s privacy, brand, and competitive advantage.

Article 9 — Ownership & Intellectual Property

9.1 Final Ownership

Once full payment is complete, the client legally owns the final delivered product.

9.2 Source Code & Internal Systems

The Agency retains ownership of all source code, internal systems, methods, techniques, frameworks, components, structures, and proprietary tools. The client owns the final result, not the underlying code as independent assets.

9.3 Code Usage Restrictions
  • The client cannot reuse the Agency’s code in other projects, platforms, or systems without permission.
  • The Agency cannot remove or restrict its own code from the final website once payment is complete.
9.4 Open-Source Rights

If the client wants full source rights, this requires a specific paid upgrade and explicit approval.

Article 10 — Client Responsibilities

10.1 Content Ownership

The client must ensure they legally own or have rights to all materials provided.

10.2 Timely Communication
  • The client must reply to requests for information within 7 days.
  • The client must approve or reject deliverables within 3 days.

Failure to respond pauses the project automatically.

10.3 Accuracy

The client is responsible for verifying the accuracy of all provided content.

Article 11 — Liability

The Agency is not responsible for:

  • Hosting issues, server downtime, platform outages, or third-party service errors.
  • Any issues caused after the client edits, modifies, or manages the website.
  • Breaches or vulnerabilities unless the client is on an active maintenance plan.
  • Any legal consequences arising from materials the client submits.

Article 12 — Cancellations

12.1 Client-Initiated Cancellation

If the client cancels the project: The 50% deposit is forfeited, and work stops immediately.

12.2 Agency-Initiated Cancellation

The Agency reserves the right to cancel the project due to abuse, violation of terms, lack of cooperation, non-payment, or illegal requests. In such cases, all completed work is retained by the Agency until full payment.

Article 13 — Disputes & Governing Law

13.1 International Jurisdiction

These Terms apply internationally, regardless of the client’s location.

13.2 Dispute Process

Any dispute must be addressed through: Email communication first, a structured review of the project, written documentation, and proposed resolutions. Both parties agree to attempt resolution in good faith before considering escalation.

Article 14 — Acceptance of Terms

By engaging with Lucient Creative, the client confirms they have read, understood, and agreed to all Terms listed here.
